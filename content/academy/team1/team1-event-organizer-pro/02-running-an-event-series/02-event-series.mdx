## Running an event series

A series is not “the same flyer with a new date.” It is a **promise** that people can plan around: similar quality bar, recognizable brand, and compounding social proof. Ecosystem playbooks for community-led programs often stress **repeatable templates** and local chapter energy over one hero organizer. Borrow that structure for Team1 without losing manual compliance.

### Pick a cadence you can keep

| Cadence | Fits when | Watch out for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Monthly anchor | Active city, student term rhythm | Fatigue if every month is max effort |
| Biweekly micro + quarterly big | You have a job or studies | Confusion if “big” and “small” feel unrelated |
| Seasonal sprint (6 to 8 weeks) | Hackathon or university block | Need a clear finale so people sense closure |

Whatever you pick, **under-promise in public**. If you can realistically do six great nights a year, say that plainly. Empty hype trains burn trust faster than quiet consistency.

### Naming and identity

Keep a stable **series name** plus a **subtitle** that rotates:

- Stable: “Avalanche Builders [City]”
- Rotating: “Session 04: custom L1s night”

Stable naming helps RSVPs, partner inboxes, and your own Drive folders. Rotating subtitles keep the story fresh and give you honest hooks for social posts.

### The runbook stack (minimum viable)

Create one folder you reuse every cycle. Minimum files:

1. **Run-of-show master** (timeboxed, roles in the margin).
2. **Venue and AV checklist** (what failed last time goes at the top).
3. **Partner comms snippets** (three emails: invite, reminder, thank-you with recap link).
4. **Volunteer one-pager** (arrival time, dress, tasks they own, escalation path for conduct issues).
5. **Content capture list** (five shots you always want, plus who holds the camera).

After each event, spend **fifteen minutes** updating the runbook while memory is hot. That is how series organizers buy back weekends.

### Asset reuse without looking stale

| Asset | Reuse tactic |
| --- | --- |
| Slides | Swap only the “what’s new in Avalanche” section each month |
| Demos | Same repo tag, new exercise page or branch |
| RSVP page | Clone Luma or Eventbrite template, change copy block 2 only |
| Swag table layout | Photo from last time as setup reference |

Builder Hub courses (for example [Avalanche Fundamentals](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/avalanche-fundamentals)) are your **canonical technical depth**. In a series, link out instead of rebuilding decks from scratch unless you have a good reason.

### De-risk the middle episodes

Episode two is often the hardest: hype from the launch is gone, routines are not muscle memory yet.

**Tactics:**

- **Lower scope** on the second date (shorter program, same venue, known MC).
- **Carry one storyline forward** (“last time we deployed X, tonight we wire the UI”).
- **Lock the venue block** for three dates up front so logistics stop stealing creative time.

### Questions to think through

1. **Cadence:** What rhythm can you defend for two quarters without heroic overtime?
2. **Template debt:** Which document caused the most scramble last time, and what is the one field you will pre-fill forever?
3. **Finale:** What is the “season ender” moment that lets you rest, report, and reset expectations?
4. **Overlap:** If another series appears in your city, will you merge, alternate months, or specialize topics?
