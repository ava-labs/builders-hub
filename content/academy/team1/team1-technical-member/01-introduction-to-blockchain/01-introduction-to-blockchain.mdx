This course assumes you are already comfortable with the fundamentals of blockchain technology. Before continuing, make sure you have completed — or can confidently answer questions from — the **Blockchain Fundamentals** course in the Avalanche Academy.

<Callout>
  If you haven't done it yet, complete **Blockchain Fundamentals** at [build.avax.network/academy/blockchain/blockchain-fundamentals](https://build.avax.network/academy/blockchain/blockchain-fundamentals) before moving on.
</Callout>

## What You Should Already Know

After completing the Blockchain Fundamentals course you should be able to:

- **Describe what a blockchain is** — a distributed, append-only ledger secured by cryptography and consensus, and relate it to real-world use cases like payments, NFTs, DeFi, gaming, and identity.
- **Explain why blockchain was created** — connecting concepts like the double-spend problem, trust minimization, and censorship resistance to the history of Bitcoin and later networks.
- **Describe consensus mechanisms** — what consensus is and how common mechanisms like Proof of Work and Proof of Stake allow distributed nodes to agree on the state of the ledger.
- **Differentiate public and private blockchains** — who can read, write, and validate in each model.
- **Explain keys, addresses, and wallets** — the difference between a private key, public key, and address, and how wallets manage keys and sign transactions.
- **Describe how blocks and mempools work** — the structure of a block and the path a transaction takes from broadcast to inclusion.

If any of these topics feel unfamiliar, revisit the relevant lessons before continuing. The sections that follow build directly on this knowledge.
