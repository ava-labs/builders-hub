## What You Should Already Know

To be a technical member, after completing Avalanche Fundamentals you should be able to:

- **Explain what Avalanche is** — a multi-chain platform with a primary network consisting of the X-Chain, P-Chain, and C-Chain.
- **Define what an L1 is** — understanding that Avalanche L1s have sovereign validator sets and do not post proofs to a parent chain.
- **Compare Avalanche, Ethereum, and L2s** — the architectural and scalability differences, including Avalanche's multi-chain design versus Ethereum's single mainnet plus L2 ecosystem.
- **Explain vertical vs. horizontal scalability** — vertical scaling improves a single chain; horizontal scaling adds more chains to distribute load.
- **Describe Avalanche consensus** — the repeated random subsampling approach that achieves fast, probabilistic finality with high safety margins, and how it differs from classical Proof of Stake.
- **Explain finality on Avalanche** — why Avalanche can achieve finality in 1-2 seconds.
- **Name several live L1 blockchains** — Avalanche, Ethereum, Bitcoin, Solana, and others, and describe their main architectural trade-offs.
- **Know what can be customized in an Avalanche L1** — VM, tokenomics, permissioning, fee structure, and precompiles.

If any of these topics feel unfamiliar, return to the Avalanche Fundamentals course before continuing. Sections 3 through 6 of this course assume fluency with Avalanche architecture and the EVM.
