## Section 2 – Introduction to Avalanche and Its Ecosystem

Every Team1 member should also be able to explain how Avalanche works at a high level and why it is different.  
The **Avalanche Fundamentals** course on Builder Hub covers consensus, multi‑chain architecture, and L1s in detail.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/avalanche-fundamentals`](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/avalanche-fundamentals)

**By the end of this section you should be able to:**

- **Explain what Avalanche is and what the C‑Chain is**
  - Difference between the Avalanche network as a multi‑chain platform and the **Avalanche C‑Chain**, which is an EVM‑compatible chain within the primary network.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/avalanche-fundamentals`](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/avalanche-fundamentals)

- **Define what an L1 is**
  - Explain L1 blockchains as base settlement and execution layers, and contrast them with L2s that sit on top of L1s.

- **Compare Avalanche, Ethereum, and L2s**
  - Explain architectural and scalability differences, including Avalanche’s multi‑chain design vs. Ethereum’s single mainnet plus L2 rollups.

- **Explain vertical vs. horizontal scalability**
  - Vertical: scaling a single chain with more powerful hardware.
  - Horizontal: adding more chains or subnets to distribute load.

- **Explain how Avalanche consensus differs from typical Proof of Stake**
  - High‑level description of the repeated random subsampling approach and how it leads to fast, probabilistic consensus with high safety margins.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/avalanche-fundamentals`](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/avalanche-fundamentals)

- **Describe Avalanche architecture and the roles of X‑, P‑, and C‑Chains**
  - X‑Chain for asset transfers, P‑Chain for validators and subnets, C‑Chain for EVM smart contracts in the primary network.
  - Why the **P‑Chain** is critical for interoperability and subnet management.

- **Explain finality and why Avalanche can match block production time**
  - Why Avalanche’s consensus plus its networking model allows finality to be effectively equal to the block production time rather than many blocks later.

- **Know where to find stats about existing L1s**
  - Use Avalanche explorer and ecosystem dashboards to see active L1s and their metrics.[`https://build.avax.network`](https://build.avax.network)

- **Name at least five L1s and describe their focus**
  - Examples: Avalanche (multi‑chain, custom L1s), Ethereum (general‑purpose smart contracts), Bitcoin (sound money), Solana (high‑throughput monolithic chain), etc.

- **Explain what can be customized in an Avalanche L1**
  - Parameters like virtual machine, tokenomics, permissioning, and precompiles, as covered in L1 and Customizing EVM academy tracks.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1`](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1)
