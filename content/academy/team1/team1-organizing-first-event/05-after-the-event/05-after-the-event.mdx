## 4. After the Event

Many communities fail to capture the value of an event because they skip structured follow‑up.  
Builder Hub’s event‑planning guidance explicitly calls out **forgetting follow‑up and feedback** as a common mistake.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community`](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community)  
Team1’s structures for bounties, XP, and reporting depend on organizers submitting summaries and proof of impact, as documented in the manual.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)

### 4.1 Event Report & Metrics

**Checklist – Internal Report**

- Summarize:
  - Event format and audience.
  - Number of RSVPs vs. actual attendees.
  - Key highlights (notable builders, partners, questions).
- Attach:
  - Photos or screenshots (ideally a small set that clearly shows the space and audience).
  - Short video clips if available (walkthrough, speaker moments, attendee reactions).
  - Links to slides, recordings, or threads.
- Reflect:
  - What worked well?
  - What you would change next time?
  - Any follow‑up opportunities (new partnerships, workshop requests, hackathon ideas).

### 4.2 Community Follow‑Up

**Checklist – External Follow‑Up**

- Share:
  - Slides, resources, and relevant links in the same channels you used for promotion.
  - A short recap post in Team1 Discord and/or social media, **always including at least a couple of good photos or a short video clip** if participants and venue rules allow, following community guidelines.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)
- Invite:
  - Attendees to join a follow‑up call, builders’ meetup, or next workshop.
  - Feedback via a simple form or message, to help improve future events.

**Example KPIs for Your Report**

When you fill in the internal report and any official post‑event questionnaire linked from the Team1 Manual or onboarding hub, consider tracking.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)

- **Attendance metrics**
  - RSVPs vs. check‑ins
  - New vs. returning attendees
- **Engagement metrics**
  - Questions asked, time spent in Q&A
  - Number of people who joined relevant Discord channels or mailing lists after the event
- **Content & reach**
  - Social media posts and impressions related to the event
  - Views or downloads of slides/recordings
- **Conversion metrics**
  - Builders who started a project, applied for a program, or signed up for a follow‑up workshop

### 4.3 Integrating Learnings into Team1

Team1’s documentation and blog emphasize learning from each activation to improve future initiatives and share playbooks across regions.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual) [`https://www.team1.blog`](https://www.team1.blog)

**Checklist – Closing the Loop**

- Post:
  - A short debrief in the relevant Team1 channels with key takeaways.
- Update:
  - Any internal templates or checklists you used with improvements.
- Consider:
  - Turning your experience into a **Team1 blog post** or internal guide, so other members can replicate or adapt your format.[`https://www.team1.blog`](https://www.team1.blog)
