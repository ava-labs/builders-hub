## Section 8 – Encrypted ERC (eERC)

Encrypted ERC (eERC) enables privacy‑preserving tokens on Avalanche by combining cryptographic primitives (Zero‑Knowledge Proofs, partially homomorphic encryption, etc.) with ERC‑compatible interfaces. This topic is covered in the **Encrypted ERC** course within the Blockchain track on Builder Hub.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/blockchain/encrypted-erc`](https://build.avax.network/academy/blockchain/encrypted-erc)

### 8.1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this section, you should be able to:

- Define **what an encrypted token is** and how it differs from a standard ERC‑20/721/1155 token.
- Explain:
  - What the **BabyJubJub** elliptic curve is and why it is useful for zk‑friendly cryptography.
  - How BabyJubJub differs from schemes like **BLS** in terms of use cases and properties.
- Describe:
  - **Symmetric cryptography** (same key for encryption and decryption).
  - **Asymmetric cryptography** (public/private key pairs).
  - **Zero‑Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs)** and why they are critical for private balances and transfers.
  - **Partial Homomorphic Encryption** and its properties relevant to encrypted balances and operations.
- Understand the roles of **PCT** and **EGCT** (as defined in the eERC standard) and how they participate in confidential transactions.
- Distinguish between tokens created with **Standalone** and **Converter** eERC flows and when each is appropriate.
- Describe the **process to initialize an eERC contract** (Standalone or Converter) on Avalanche.
- Describe the **process to initialize a user** in an eERC contract (key generation, registration, and any necessary setup).
- Differentiate between **Mint** and **Deposit**, and give examples of when each is used.
- Explain **how balances are stored on‑chain** for eERC tokens and how they differ from plain ERC‑20 balances.
- Identify **which ZK proofs** are used to generate transfer signatures in eERC and what properties they guarantee (e.g., balance conservation, no over‑spending).
- Differentiate between **Burn** and **Withdraw**, and describe use cases for each within the eERC model.
- List at least **three real‑world use cases** of encrypted tokens (e.g., private payroll, confidential DeFi positions, privacy‑preserving identity or ticketing).
