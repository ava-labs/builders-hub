## Section 6 – Custom L1s on Avalanche

Avalanche makes it possible to launch custom L1s with tailored tokenomics, permissioning, and precompiles.  
Builder Hub’s L1 courses (Permissionless L1s, Permissioned L1s, L1 Native Tokenomics, Customizing the EVM, etc.) cover these topics in depth.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1`](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1)

**By the end of this section you should be able to:**

- Explain:
  - What an **L1** is and how it differs from an L2 (base vs. layered execution).
  - What a **node** is and its role in consensus and validation.
- Know:
  - The minimum number of nodes required to run a production‑ready Avalanche subnet / blockchain, based on current Avalanche documentation and best practices.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/permissionless-l1s`](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/permissionless-l1s)
  - The minimum uptime required for node rewards according to Avalanche governance (ACP‑267 raises primary network validator uptime requirements from 80% to 90% for rewards eligibility).[`https://build.avax.network/academy`](https://build.avax.network/academy)
- Understand roles & configuration:
  - Difference between **admin**, **manager**, and **enabled** roles in custom L1 configuration, as described in L1 and access‑restriction courses.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/access-restriction`](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/access-restriction)
  - What **precompiles** are and how they extend EVM behavior at the protocol level.
- Understand Avalanche Warp Messaging (AWM):
  - High‑level purpose of **AWM** as an interoperability primitive for L1s on Avalanche.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/interchain-messaging`](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/interchain-messaging)
- Know the purpose of key precompiles:
  - `deployerAllowListConfig`
  - `nativeMinterConfig`
  - `txAllowListConfig`
  - `feeManagerConfig`
  - `rewardManagerConfig`
  - `warpConfig`
  and how they relate to permissioning, token minting, fees, rewards, and messaging in a custom L1.

- Give **examples of custom L1s**:
  - Describe at least a few live L1s built on Avalanche (e.g., app‑specific chains, gaming chains, DeFi‑focused chains) and what makes them unique, drawing from case studies in Builder Hub and Team1 content.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1`](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1)

**Suggested learning path**

- Complete the **Permissionless L1s**, **Permissioned L1s**, and related L1 courses on Builder Hub.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1`](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1)
- Follow along with a Team1 workshop or bootcamp module that walks through creating and configuring a custom L1, similar to the privacy‑focused chain exercises in the Latam Bootcamp.[`https://team1-latam-bootcamp.gitbook.io/team1-docs`](https://team1-latam-bootcamp.gitbook.io/team1-docs)
