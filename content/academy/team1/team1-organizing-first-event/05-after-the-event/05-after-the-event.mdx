Many communities fail to capture the value of an event because they skip structured follow-up. 

### Event report, recap form, and reimbursement

After the event, a **Post Event Report** is expected.

**Event success report**

- Brief written summary, attendance versus RSVPs, and KPIs.
- Links to **social posts** from the event.
- Onboarding signals where applicable: Discord joins, **Core Wallet**, **Avalanche Academy**, Builder Hub signups, and so on. The manual calls these out as useful data points (same page as above).
- **Official event recap form:** complete within **48 hours** of the event ([Notion recap form](https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/25e688ec20c48064a093dcf7c613c36b)).

**Checklist: evidence bundle**

- Summarize format, audience, RSVPs versus check-ins, and standout moments (builders met, partnerships, follow-up leads).
- Attach a **tight** photo set or short clips that show the room and energy (respecting venue and attendee consent).
- Link slides, recordings, and threads; note anything promised for follow-up (office hours, next workshop).
- Reflect: what worked, what you would change, and one concrete **next event** or **community** action.

### Community follow-up

**Checklist: external follow-up**

- Share:
  - Slides, resources, and relevant links in the same channels you used for promotion.
  - A short recap post in Team1 Discord and/or social media, **including at least a couple of good photos or a short video clip** when participants and venue rules allow, following community guidelines.
- Invite:
  - Attendees to join a follow-up call, builders’ meetup, or next workshop.
  - Feedback via a simple form or message, to help improve future events.

**Example KPIs for your report**

When you fill in the internal report and any official post-event questionnaire from the manual or onboarding hub, consider tracking:

- **Attendance metrics:** RSVPs versus check-ins; new versus returning attendees.
- **Engagement metrics:** questions asked, time spent in Q&A; people who joined relevant Discord channels or mailing lists after the event.
- **Content and reach:** social posts and impressions related to the event; views or downloads of slides and recordings.
- **Conversion metrics:** builders who started a project, applied for a program, or signed up for a follow-up workshop.

### Integrating learnings into Team1

We are learning from each activation to improve future initiatives and share playbooks across regions, so please be welcome to share any kind of experience you've had and mention specially if there's something you've learned or realized we could be doing better.

**Checklist: closing the loop**

- Post a short debrief in the relevant Team1 channels with key takeaways.
- Update any internal templates or checklists you used with improvements.
- Consider turning your experience into a **Team1 blog post** or internal guide so other members can replicate or adapt your format ([Team1 blog](https://www.team1.blog)).

### Questions to think through

1. **Recap:** Can you complete the Notion recap form honestly inside 48 hours with the proof you already collected?
2. **Community:** What is the single best follow-up channel for your audience (Discord, Telegram, university mailing list)?
3. **Learning:** What is the one thing you would never run an event without again, or what failed once but is fixable next time?
