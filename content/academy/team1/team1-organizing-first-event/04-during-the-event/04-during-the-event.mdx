## 3. During the Event

With good prep, the event itself is about execution, energy, and responsiveness.  
The event‑planning lesson calls out the importance of a clear **run‑of‑show**, quality AV, and designing the environment (lighting, food, music) to create the right emotional impact.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community`](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community)

### 3.1 Host Responsibilities

**Checklist – At the Start**

- Arrive early:
  - Test AV and internet.
  - Set up signage, check‑in desk, and any swag.
- Brief your team:
  - Who is greeting people at the door?
  - Who is watching time and keeping the agenda on track?
  - Who is the point‑of‑contact for venue or security staff?
- Welcome attendees:
  - Short introduction of Avalanche, Team1, and event goals, using language consistent with official descriptions and values.[`https://www.avax.network/ambassador`](https://www.avax.network/ambassador) [`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)

### 3.2 Running the Program

**Checklist – During Talks / Workshops**

- Stick to the run‑of‑show:
  - Start and end segments on time where possible.
  - Clearly signal transitions between talks, demos, and networking.
- Support speakers:
  - Help with slides / demos.
  - Repeat questions if audio is unclear.
- Encourage interaction:
  - Ask for questions and feedback.
  - Facilitate intros between attendees (e.g., builders and potential collaborators).

### 3.3 Community Care & Content Capture

The event‑planning lesson encourages designing for content creation (photos, video, social clips) and gathering feedback during and after the event.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community`](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community)  
Team1’s bounty and XP systems often ask for evidence of impact, so documenting your event is important.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)

**Checklist – Care & Documentation**

- Ensure:
  - Code of conduct is respected; address any issues quietly and promptly.
  - Organizers are visible and approachable for questions or concerns.
- Capture:
  - Photos or short video clips (respecting venue / participant rules).
  - Basic stats: check‑ins, peak attendance, notable conversations or outcomes.
- Seed follow‑up:
  - Mention where slides, recordings, or links will be shared.
  - Encourage people to join relevant Discord channels or sign up for follow‑up sessions.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)
