## Module 6 – Getting Started: Your First 30 Days

### 6.1 Application & Joining

The current status of applications, cohorts, and waitlists is always maintained on the **official Avalanche Team1 / Ambassador page**.[`https://www.avax.network/ambassador`](https://www.avax.network/ambassador)

Rather than duplicating that information here, this course will always link you back to:

- The **latest application form**.
- Any announcements about new cohorts or special programs.

Always refer to that page for the most up-to-date process.

### 6.2 Onboarding Flow

Once accepted, new members go through a structured **onboarding journey** documented in the **Team1 Member Onboarding** Notion.[`https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/Team1-Member-Onboarding-269688ec20c4808fb3ece079485f0441`](https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/Team1-Member-Onboarding-269688ec20c4808fb3ece079485f0441)

Typical steps include:

1. **Reading the Team1 Manual** to understand how the program works, what’s expected, and where to find information.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)
2. **Watching onboarding / orientation videos** that introduce the program, key people, and current priorities.
3. **Joining Discord** and introducing yourself in the designated channels.
4. **Reviewing active bounties and initiatives**, so you can quickly find something meaningful to work on.

These steps are meant to be completed within your **first few weeks** to help you become fully integrated into the community.

### 6.3 Designing Your First Contribution

Drawing from examples in the Team1 Manual and Blog, good first contributions include:[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual) [`https://www.team1.blog`](https://www.team1.blog)

- Helping **promote an upcoming Avalanche or Team1 event** in your local community or online.
- Attending an existing event and taking on a supporting role (check-in, logistics, moderation).
- Writing a **short educational thread or blog post** explaining a core Avalanche concept in your own language.
- Participating in a **working group call** and volunteering for a small but concrete task.
