## Introduction – Why Events Matter in Team1

Events are one of the main ways Team1 grows the Avalanche ecosystem.  
The official Avalanche Team1 / Ambassador page highlights that Team1 members organize workshops, hackathons, meetups, and other in‑person and online activities to “grow Avalanche” around the world.[`https://www.avax.network/ambassador`](https://www.avax.network/ambassador)  
The Avalanche Team1 Manual (GitBook) goes deeper, explaining that running events is a core contribution type for members and providing guidance on how to do so responsibly and effectively.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)

Builder Hub’s **Event Planning for Community Building** lesson shows how Ava Labs approaches strategic events more broadly: start from a clear “why,” define your audience, create a run‑of‑show, and design for emotional impact.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community`](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community)  
This course adapts those principles specifically to Team1, in the context of the expectations and structures described in Team1 documentation and onboarding materials.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual) [`https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/Team1-Member-Onboarding-269688ec20c4808fb3ece079485f0441`](https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/Team1-Member-Onboarding-269688ec20c4808fb3ece079485f0441)

### Common Types of Team1 Events

Across the Team1 Manual, onboarding resources, and public communications (blog posts and announcements), Team1 consistently highlights several recurring event formats.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual) [`https://www.team1.blog`](https://www.team1.blog) [`https://www.avax.network/ambassador`](https://www.avax.network/ambassador)

- **Introductory meetups** – local gatherings to introduce Avalanche, Team1, and current ecosystem opportunities.
- **University workshops & lectures** – sessions with student clubs and university partners, often hands‑on or lecture‑style.
- **Builder / developer meetups** – more technical events covering tooling, smart contracts, L1s, or ecosystem projects.
- **Hackathons & build sprints** – short, intense building experiences, sometimes tied to broader Avalanche hackathons or Codebase initiatives.
- **Online webinars / Twitter Spaces / Discord events** – remote sessions that can reach communities beyond a single city.

This course assumes you are preparing **any of these formats** and focuses on the process: making a proposal, preparing, executing, and following up.
