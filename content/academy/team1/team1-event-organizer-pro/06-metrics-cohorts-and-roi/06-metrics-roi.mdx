## Metrics, cohorts, and ROI

“ROI” for community events is not a finance meme. It is the answer to: **did the ecosystem get stronger because we ran this?** Headcount can lie. Repeat attendance, shipped projects, and people helping the next cohort usually tell a truer story.

You still file Team1 recaps on time ([recap form](https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/25e688ec20c48064a093dcf7c613c36b), manual guidance on [during and post event](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual/how-to-run-an-event/steps-to-running-your-own-community-event/what-to-do-during-and-post-event)). This module is about **what to collect** so your future proposals and partner pitches write themselves.

### Leading versus lagging indicators

| Type | Examples | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Leading (early) | RSVPs to ratio, show rate, workshop repo clones | Lets you fix promo and format fast |
| Lagging (late) | Repeat attendees, projects demoed, grants applied | Proves stickiness months later |

Track at least **one of each** per season. If you only track lagging, you fly blind mid-season. If you only track leading, you celebrate busy rooms that never convert into builders.

### Cohort view (lightweight)

A cohort is just “people who started with us in the same window.” Even a spreadsheet works.

**Minimum columns:**

1. First touch date (event ID).
2. Skill tag (student, Solidity curious, L1 dev, and so on).
3. Return count in ninety days.
4. One “output” field (PR opened, hackathon team formed, Discord helper shift, empty if none).

You are not building a surveillance machine. You are building **memory** so you stop re-asking the same onboarding questions.

### Honest reporting beats hero stats

Programs that last reward honesty. If a workshop had low completion, say so and note the fix (Wi-Fi, difficulty curve, starter repo). If a partner push flopped, write that down before you repeat the partnership shape.

**Vanity traps to avoid as primary KPIs:**

- Raw Telegram member adds with no engagement plan.
- “Impressions” without a second step you care about.
- Speaker count as a substitute for attendee outcomes.

**Stronger secondary KPIs (pick a few):**

- **Show rate** (checked in divided by RSVPs).
- **Workshop completion** (tasks finished divided by attendees who started).
- **Return rate** (people at event N who were at N-1).
- **Builder Hub click-through** from your unique links or pinned recap paths.

UTM parameters on links are optional but useful when you control the landing page.

### Translating outcomes for partners

When a sponsor or university asks “what did we get?”, answer with a **triple**:

1. **Reach:** headcount plus rough demographics if you have them.
2. **Depth:** completions, repos, projects, office hours booked.
3. **Narrative:** one quote or clip you are allowed to share.

That structure matches how many web3 education and community teams report public impact without overselling.

### Questions to think through

1. **North star lagging metric:** What single lagging metric would make you proud if it moved slowly but surely over a year?
2. **Instrument:** What is the cheapest honest way to track repeat attendance without creepy data?
3. **Drop-off:** Where do people usually disappear in your funnel (RSVP, show, stay through workshop, come back)?
4. **Recap upgrade:** What is one extra field you will add to your personal recap notes that official forms might not capture but helps you improve?
