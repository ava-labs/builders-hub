The Avalanche Octane upgrade activates ACP-176, positioning the C-Chain for **cheaper gas fees and better handling of future increases in network demand**. This upgrade introduces two major improvements: enhanced dynamic fee mechanisms and validator-controlled gas target voting.

**Testnet Release**: v1.13.0-fuji
**Testnet Activation**: March 13, 2025 at 11:00 AM ET (Fuji)
**Mainnet Release**: v1.13.0
**Mainnet Activation**: TBD (Requires v1.13.0 upgrade before deadline)

## Improving Dynamic Fee Mechanisms

### Current C-Chain Fee System

Currently, the C-Chain uses a static 15,000,000 gas target per 10-second window with a modified EIP-1559 mechanism. While functional, this system can cause **large spikes in gas prices following large blocks**.

### Enhanced Fee Algorithm

The upgrade implements the mechanism from ACP-103 to improve fee handling:

- **Smoother Price Adjustments**: Reduces volatility in gas price fluctuations
- **Better Congestion Management**: More gradual response to network load changes
- **Predictable Costs**: Users experience more stable transaction fees

### Community Validation

Gas fee simulations were performed and discussed publicly in developer community calls, with results publicly shared. The community-driven approach ensures the new mechanism addresses real-world usage patterns.

## Validator Gas Target Voting

### Dynamic Network Scaling

Previously, adjusting target gas consumption required network upgrades. ACP-176 enables validators to dynamically adjust targets based on their preferences and hardware capabilities, allowing the network to **scale more effectively in response to varying loads**.

### How It Works

**Validator Preferences**: Each validator can signal their preferred gas target based on:
- Hardware capacity
- Network conditions
- Resource availability

**Consensus-Based Adjustment**: The network aggregates validator preferences to determine optimal gas targets without requiring hard forks.

**Responsive Scaling**: Gas targets can adjust to accommodate:
- Increasing transaction demand
- Complex smart contract operations
- Evolving ecosystem needs

### Benefits

1. **No Hard Fork Required**: Gas limit adjustments happen through validator consensus
2. **Hardware-Aware**: Validators with stronger hardware can support higher targets
3. **Decentralized Control**: Community-driven scaling decisions
4. **Future-Proof**: Adaptive mechanism grows with ecosystem demands

## Technical Implementation

### ACP-176: Validator-Adjustable Gas Target

The core proposal enables:
- **Vote Mechanism**: Validators signal preferred gas targets
- **Weighted Aggregation**: Stake-weighted voting determines network-wide target
- **Smooth Transitions**: Gradual adjustments prevent disruption

### ACP-103 Fee Mechanism

Improved dynamic fee algorithm provides:
- **Exponential Smoothing**: Reduces price volatility
- **Better Responsiveness**: Faster adaptation to sustained load changes
- **Lower Spikes**: Mitigates extreme price jumps
