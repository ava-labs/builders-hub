## Before the event

Once your proposal is approved, it is time to work in full preparation.

### Logistics and run-of-show

**Checklist: logistics**

- **Venue / platform confirmed**
  - Venue booking or online platform created.
  - Capacity known and appropriate for your target attendance.
- **Run-of-show drafted**
  - Timeline from setup through doors open, content, networking, teardown.
  - Who is responsible for each segment (host, speaker, MC, support).
- **Equipment and materials**
  - AV needs (microphones, speakers, projector, adapters). The Builder Hub event lesson calls out AV quality as a common failure point and recommends prioritizing it (same lesson link as above).
  - Signage, check-in desk, nametags, swag.

### Content and speakers

It is important you provide accurate, up-to-date information when talking about Avalanche and encourage using official decks and resources where possible (don't be afraid to ask other team1 members!). Builder Hub’s [Avalanche Fundamentals](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/avalanche-fundamentals) and [Blockchain](https://build.avax.network/academy/blockchain) tracks provide canonical technical explanations you can reuse or reference.

**Checklist: content prep**

- Choose or adapt:
  - Official Avalanche or Team1 introduction slides (from internal resources or manual and onboarding references).
  - Technical content (if any) that matches your audience’s level.
- Confirm speakers:
  - Bios, talk titles, and timings confirmed.
  - They understand **Team1 values** and community guidelines (manual).
- Prepare:
  - Q&A prompts and backup questions.
  - Demo environments or testnets ready if you are doing live demos.

### Promotion and RSVPs

Partnering with other communities and using platforms like Luma or Eventbrite for RSVPs, and tools like Galxe or Zealy for gamified engagement could be good things to have in mind for promotion and RSVPs.

**Checklist: promotion**

- Create:
  - Event landing page or RSVP link.
  - Visual assets following Avalanche and Team1 brand guidelines.
- Share via:
  - Team1 Discord channels relevant to your region or focus area.
  - Partner communities, university groups, or local builders.
  - Social media, including official Team1 channels when appropriate and approved. 
- Plan:
  - Two or three reminder posts leading up to the event.
  - A clear description of what people will **actually get** from attending (skills, connections, opportunities).

### Compliance, safety, and accessibility

Team1 values safe, inclusive, and welcoming environments.

**Checklist: safety and access**

- Confirm:
  - Any necessary permissions with venue, university, or partners.
  - A clear **code of conduct** (Team1 and Avalanche guidelines) and that organizers know how to enforce it (manual).
- Consider:
  - Accessibility (stairs versus elevator, quiet spaces, online access, language).
  - Dietary needs if providing food.

### Additional requirements for technical workshops

The Team1 Manual includes **minimum requirements** for technical workshops sponsored and supported through Team1. They help participants follow along and keep content aligned with Avalanche priorities ([Team1 Manual](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)).

**Topic and audience requirements**

- Have a **community interested in a specific technical topic**, such as:
  - Introduction to blockchain
  - Introduction to Avalanche and its ecosystem
  - Introduction to Solidity
  - Custom L1s
  - Interoperability with ICM and ICTT
  - Encrypted tokens with eERC
- For these topics, point learners to **[Builder Hub](https://build.avax.network)** for asynchronous content that complements the workshop, as the manual recommends.

**Venue and infrastructure requirements**

- Secure a **partnership or venue** suitable for a hands-on workshop, ideally:
  - A classroom or room with tables for laptops
  - Enough **electrical outlets** for attendees
  - A **stable internet connection** for everyone following the exercises

**Instructor, marketing, and materials**

- At least **one technical instructor with demonstrable knowledge** of the chosen topic, as the manual requires for supported technical workshops.
- A clear **marketing strategy** to attract participants and support attendance (local communities, universities, online audiences).
- **Educational materials** prepared in advance:
  - Slides or written guides
  - Repos, starter kits, or example code (Solidity, custom L1s, ICM/ICTT, eERC, and so on)
  - Links to relevant Builder Hub courses (for example Avalanche Fundamentals, Custom L1s, Interoperability, Encrypted ERC).

### Questions to think through

1. **Failure modes:** If Wi-Fi or the projector fails ten minutes before start, what is your backup plan and who executes it?
2. **Audience level:** Can your weakest plausible attendee still follow, or only your strongest speaker’s ideal listener?
3. **Proof:** Where will you store RSVPs, check-in counts, and photo links so the 48-hour post-event recap is painless?
4. **Conduct:** Who is the designated person to handle a code-of-conduct concern calmly and off the main stage?
