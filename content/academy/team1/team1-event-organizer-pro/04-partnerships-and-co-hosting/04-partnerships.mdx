Partnerships in web3 often fail for boring reasons: unclear ownership of RSVPs, mismatched effort, or “co-host” meaning **two logos and one exhausted organizer**. Good co-production looks like a **small contract without lawyers**: scope, deliverables, timeline, and a single shared RSVP surface.

Everything still routes through Team1’s official proposal and recap flow ([Team1 Manual](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual), [proposal form](https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/215688ec20c48074bb59c0212dbd74d0)). Partners do not replace program accountability. They multiply reach when roles are explicit.

### Before you DM anyone

Answer internally:

| Question | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| What do they bring that you cannot? | Audience, venue, cred, language access, media |
| What do you bring that they cannot? | Avalanche story, Team1 ops, Builder Hub depth |
| What is the single shared KPI? | Stops vague “awareness” partnerships |
| Who owns the run-of-show? | Prevents double MC and double silence |

If the honest answer is “we both want the same fifty people,” merge events or **alternate months** instead of competing.

### Co-host package (copy-paste skeleton)

Send a short note with six bullets:

1. **Working title + date options** (two choices max).
2. **Audience** (level, language, expected headcount band).
3. **Format** (panels, workshop, hybrid, and so on).
4. **Split of labor** with names: promotion, check-in, AV, content capture.
5. **Brand rules** (where official Avalanche and Team1 marks appear, link to manual or internal brand notes).
6. **Success signal** you will both mention in the recap (not vanity alone).

### Win-win structures that actually happen

| Pattern | When it shines |
| --- | --- |
| **Venue partner** | They donate space, you bring program and speakers |
| **University club** | They handle room booking, you bring curriculum + swag path via program |
| **Protocol or DAO** | They bring specialist speaker, you anchor local crowd and logistics |
| **Media partner** | They commit to two posts + live coverage, you give stage shout-outs and recap tags |

Avoid “partners” who only want a **stage slot** with no list share and no prep call. Politely ask for one tangible asset (mailing push, Discord announcement with date pinned, or staffed table).

### Deconflict with other Team1 members

If two people cover the same geography:

- **Share a calendar pin** in Discord for anchor dates.
- **Specialize topics** (one leans university, one leans senior builders) when possible.
- **Joint season** beats dueling RSVPs when audiences overlap hard.

Escalate genuine stalemates through the channels the manual and onboarding describe. Your job is to surface conflict early, not to win territory.

### Questions to think through

1. **Value exchange:** For your next partnership pitch, what is the one asset only you can bring, and the one you need from them?
2. **Risk:** What could a partner say on stage that would be inaccurate or off-brand, and how will you pre-brief to prevent it?
3. **Credit:** How will public posts split credit without cluttering every graphic with six logos?
4. **Exit:** If a partner ghosts a week out, what is your smaller solo backup format?
