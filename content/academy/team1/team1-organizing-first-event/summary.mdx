## Summary & Next Steps

In this course, you learned how to move from idea → proposal → preparation → execution → follow‑up for a Team1 event, combining Team1’s own documentation and expectations with event‑planning best practices from Builder Hub.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual) [`https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community`](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community)

Your immediate next steps are to:

- Identify the **type of event** you want to run (meetup, workshop, university session, online event, hackathon) based on local needs and your strengths.[`https://www.avax.network/ambassador`](https://www.avax.network/ambassador) [`https://www.team1.blog`](https://www.team1.blog)
- Draft a **one‑page proposal** and align it with your regional coordinator or working group.
- Use the checklists in this course, alongside the official Team1 templates and guidelines, as your backbone for planning and reporting your first Team1 event.[`https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/Team1-Member-Onboarding-269688ec20c4808fb3ece079485f0441`](https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/Team1-Member-Onboarding-269688ec20c4808fb3ece079485f0441)
