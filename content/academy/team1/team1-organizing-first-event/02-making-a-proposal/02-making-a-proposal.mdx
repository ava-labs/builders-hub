Team1 uses proposals to align events with Avalanche goals, use resources well, and set events up for success.

### Official event proposal form (submission)

Use the **official Team1 event proposal form** from the [how to create an event proposal](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual/how-to-run-an-event/steps-to-running-your-own-community-event/how-to-create-an-event-proposal) page. Do not rely on ad hoc messages or unofficial templates, or your request may not land in the expected queue.

- **Event proposal form:** [open the Notion form](https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/215688ec20c48074bb59c0212dbd74d0).
- **Typical response time:** the team aims to reach out within **48 to 72 hours** after you submit.
- **If approved:** you should receive confirmation and guidance on how to proceed.

For narrative structure, positioning, and run-of-show thinking alongside Team1’s form fields, use Builder Hub’s [Event planning for community building](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community) lesson.

### 1. Start with your “why”

You should always start with the **real** “why” of your event: who it is for, what outcomes you want, and how you will measure success.

When drafting your Team1 proposal, clearly answer:

- Why this event, in this city, community, or online space?
- Who is it for (students, devs, founders, gamers, local users)?
- What should attendees think, feel, or do when they leave?
- How does this contribute to Team1’s mission of growing the Avalanche community?

### 2. Check you have a clear idea of what the event should look like

- **Goal and success metrics defined**
  - Primary goal (for example, “onboard 20 new devs to Avalanche tooling” or “kickstart a university club”).
  - Two or three concrete metrics (attendance, new Builder Hub users, follow-up signups, Core Wallet downloads).
- **Audience and format**
  - Who the event is for (students, devs, founders, general crypto-curious, and so on).
  - Format (meetup, workshop, hackathon, online panel, office hours).
- **Program and speakers**
  - Short agenda (talks, demos, networking, Q&A).
  - Tentative speakers or facilitators (Team1 members, local builders, partners).
- **Date, time, and location**
  - Proposed date or time range and venue (or online platform).
- **Support requested**
  - What you need from Team1 or Avalanche (materials, swag, budget support, promo).
- **Risks and constraints**
  - Any known constraints (venue capacity, legal or school constraints, co-host rules).

### Internal alignment before you submit

Align with people who can help you avoid calendar clashes, set realistic budget expectations, and route questions. That is especially important for larger events, partnerships, or anything touching universities and local orgs.

**Checklist: before you submit the form**

- Share your draft with:
  - Anyone who **co-hosts**, **speaks**, or **signs** venue or school paperwork.
  - Peers in **Team1 Discord** who know your city or vertical (universities, builders, gaming, and so on).
- Confirm:
  - The event does not clash with major Avalanche or Team1 activations in your region.
  - Budget, swag, and staffing expectations match what you entered in the proposal, including **pre-approved expense lines**.
- Submit via the **official Notion event proposal form** linked above, not a free-form email, so it fits Team1’s approval and Drive workflows.

### Questions to think through

1. **Stakeholders:** Who needs to say “yes” locally (venue, university, co-host) before the program says “yes” on paper?
2. **Timing:** If approval takes up to 72 hours, what is the earliest date you can responsibly promote?
3. **Evidence:** What will you measure the day of the event so the recap form is easy to complete within 48 hours afterward?
