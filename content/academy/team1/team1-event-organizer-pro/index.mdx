<Callout>
  This course assumes you already understand proposals, prep, day-of execution, and recap from **Organizing Your First Team1 Event**. Here we focus on what changes when you run a rhythm of events and grow a small organizing system around you.
</Callout>

You know how to ship a single event. The next step is to make your work **repeatable, shareable, and legible** to partners, volunteers, and the wider Team1 program without burning yourself out.

Across web3, strong community programs lean on a few patterns you will see here: clear “why now” positioning, co-hosting instead of solo heroics, volunteer pathways that feel fair, and reporting that goes past headcount. Public playbooks from ecosystem programs (for example [community-led event guidance](https://cmxhub.com/the-community-led-event-program-playbook)) and protocol education hubs echo the same ideas: **start from outcomes, design the program, then let logistics follow**.

## What you will build in this course

| Module | You leave with |
| --- | --- |
| 1. Program mindset | A simple arc for your city or niche (not random one-offs). |
| 2. Event series | Cadence, templates, and reuse that cut prep time each cycle. |
| 3. Volunteers | Roles, onboarding, and boundaries that keep quality high. |
| 4. Partnerships | Co-produced events that actually split work and audience. |
| 5. Content flywheel | A realistic repurposing map from one IRL or online night. |
| 6. Metrics and ROI | Signals that show whether the community is **sticking**, not just showing up once. |


---

## Course modules

1. **Module 1: Program mindset (beyond one-offs)**  
   Narrative, audience ladders, and how to say no without ghosting people.
2. **Module 2: Running an event series**  
   Cadence, naming, shared assets, and runbooks so the second date is easier than the first.
3. **Module 3: Volunteers and distributed teams**  
   Briefings, shift design, recognition, and when to pause recruiting.
4. **Module 4: Partnerships and co-hosting**  
   Win-win framing, co-marketing, and de-risking awkward overlaps with other Team1 members.
5. **Module 5: Content flywheel and repurposing**  
   Turning one evening into clips, recaps, threads, and links into Builder Hub tracks.
6. **Module 6: Metrics, cohorts, and ROI**  
   Leading versus lagging indicators, honest recap data, and what “healthy” looks like over quarters.

Finish with **Summary and next steps** in the sidebar.

### Questions to think through

1. **North star:** If someone attends three of your events over six months, what skill or relationship should they have that they did not have after one?
2. **Capacity:** How many hours per month can you defend for organizing without quietly cutting corners on safety, accessibility, or accurate Avalanche messaging?
3. **Leverage:** Who is the first person you would train to run a satellite meetup under the same brand guardrails you use?
