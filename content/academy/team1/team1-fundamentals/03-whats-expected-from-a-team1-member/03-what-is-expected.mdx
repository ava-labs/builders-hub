## What’s Expected from a Team1 Member?

### Core Values & Mindset

Every Team1 member is different and can bring different forms of value to the program. However, we do have a set of founding core values that we believe every member should have in mind:

- **Integrity**: Represent Avalanche and Team1 honestly. Don’t overpromise or misrepresent the technology or programs.
- **Collaboration**: Be supportive and inclusive. Team1 works through collaboration across regions and roles.
- **Long-Term Thinking**: Focus on sustained, authentic community building rather than short-term hype or purely transactional engagement.
- **Continuous Learning**: Stay curious about Avalanche, its tooling, and the broader Web3 landscape. To be always learning and thinking is what will bring fresh ideas and creative approaches to community engagement and problem-solving.

### What Team1 Members Actually Do

Common contribution types include:

- **Event Organization & Support**  
  Plan and run meetups, workshops, hackathons, and community events. 

- **Education & Content**  
  Explain Avalanche to different audiences through talks, slide decks, written guides, videos, or social media threads.  

- **Builder & Ecosystem Support**  
  Help teams find the right programs (like Codebase, grants, or technical support) and connect them with the Avalanche ecosystem.

- **Community & University Engagement**  
  Support student clubs, community hubs, and online communities, making Avalanche knowledge accessible in different languages and regions.

However, you are **not** expected to do everything at once or even to do any of this specific things. Instead, the program encourages you to **choose focus areas** that match your interests and strengths, which might be some of these, but you're also welcome to bring new ideas. Remember you are a **member**, you are welcome to be **you**.

### Time & Commitment

We believe commitment from members should be **consistent but flexible**: members are encouraged to contribute regularly, but the exact number of hours per week depends on your capacity and role. 

To support sustainable participation, the program uses:

- **Bounties**: specific tasks and initiatives that reward contributions such as organizing an event, creating content, or supporting builders.
- **XP / Recognition Systems**: gamified or tracking mechanisms (as described in the manual) that recognize ongoing contributions and help highlight active members.

The emphasis is on **impact and reliability**, not on being online 24/7.
