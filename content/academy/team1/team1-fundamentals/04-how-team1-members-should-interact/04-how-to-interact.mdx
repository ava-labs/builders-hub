## How Team1 Members Should Interact

### Official Channels

Our primary communication and information channels are:

- **Discord**: [discord.com/invite/team1](https://discord.com/invite/team1)  The main hub for daily communication, working groups, and coordination.

- **Telegram channels**: Different channels focus on specific activities inside the ecosystem. We recommend following at least:
  - [Avalanche Academy](https://t.me/avalancheacademy)
  - [Avalanche Announcements](https://t.me/avalanche_announcements)  

- **Team1 Blog**: [team1.blog](https://www.team1.blog)  Long-form articles, deep dives, and community stories.

- **X / Twitter**: [@AvaxTeam1](https://x.com/AvaxTeam1)

As a Team1 member, you should be comfortable navigating these channels to stay informed and connected.

Additionally, there is region-specific communication going on.

### Community Guidelines & Etiquette

You should always have in mind these guidelines when interacting:

- **Respect & Inclusion**: No harassment, discrimination, or toxic behavior. Treat fellow members, builders, and community participants with respect.
- **Accuracy & Transparency**: Don’t make misleading claims about Avalanche or associated programs. If you don’t know the answer, it’s okay to say so and refer to official docs or team members.
- **Channel Discipline**: Use the right channels for the right topics (e.g., event channels for event planning, help channels for questions, region channels for local coordination).

### Collaborating Across Regions & Roles

Team1 is intentionally global and multi-role. We should always have in mind we're all pushing for the same objective.

Good collaboration practices include:

- **Co-hosting events** with other Team1 members or local partners, rather than working in isolation.
- **Sharing resources and templates** (for decks, checklists, promo materials) instead of reinventing everything from scratch.
- **Giving credit** to collaborators and recognizing contributions publicly, which the Team1 Blog often does when highlighting community initiatives.
