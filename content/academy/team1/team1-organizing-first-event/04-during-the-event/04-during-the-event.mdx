With good prep, the event itself is about execution, energy, and responsiveness.

### Host responsibilities

**Checklist: at the start**

- Arrive early:
  - Test AV and internet.
  - Set up signage, check-in desk, and any swag.
- Brief your team:
  - Who is greeting people at the door?
  - Who is watching time and keeping the agenda on track?
  - Who is the point-of-contact for venue or security staff?
- Welcome attendees:
  - Short introduction of Avalanche, Team1, and event goals, using language consistent with official descriptions.

### Running the program

**Checklist: during talks / workshops**

- Stick to the run-of-show:
  - Start and end segments on time where possible.
  - Clearly signal transitions between talks, demos, and networking.
- Support speakers:
  - Help with slides and demos.
  - Repeat questions if audio is unclear.
- Encourage interaction:
  - Ask for questions and feedback.
  - Facilitate intros between attendees (for example builders and potential collaborators).

### Community care and content capture

We encourage designing for content creation (photos, video, social clips) and gathering feedback during and after the event.

**Checklist: care and documentation**

- Ensure:
  - Code of conduct is respected; address any issues promptly.
  - Organizers are visible and approachable for questions or concerns.
- Capture:
  - Photos or short video clips (respecting venue and participant rules).
  - Basic stats: check-ins, peak attendance, notable conversations or outcomes.
  - **Assign one organizer** to own “proof pack” duties (links to posts, headcount notes, wallet or Academy mentions worth logging) so nothing critical is missing when the **48-hour** recap window starts.
- Seed follow-up:
  - Mention where slides, recordings, or links will be shared.
  - Encourage people to join relevant Discord channels or sign up for follow-up sessions.

### Questions to think through

1. **Roles:** If you step away to fix AV, who is explicitly covering the room and the clock?
2. **Energy:** What is the one moment in the run-of-show where first-time attendees should feel welcomed, not sold to?
3. **Capture:** Did you get shots of the room, a clip of applause or Q&A, and a clear note of approximate headcount for the recap?
4. **Edge cases:** How will you shorten the agenda if a speaker runs long without trashing the networking block at the end?
