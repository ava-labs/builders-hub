## 2. Before the Event

Once your proposal is approved, most of the work is in preparation.  
Builder Hub’s event‑planning lesson stresses creating a **run‑of‑show**, budget, and clear communication channels as early as possible to avoid last‑minute chaos.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community`](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community)  
Team1 documentation adds guidelines on representing Avalanche correctly, using approved materials, and coordinating with other members.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)

### 2.1 Logistics & Run‑of‑Show

**Checklist – Logistics**

- **Venue / platform confirmed**
  - Venue booking or online platform created (Discord stage, Twitter Space, Zoom, etc.).
  - Capacity known and appropriate for your target attendance.
- **Run‑of‑show drafted**
  - Timeline from setup → doors open → content → networking → teardown.
  - Who is responsible for each segment (host, speaker, MC, support).
- **Equipment & materials**
  - AV needs (microphones, speakers, projector, adapters).  
    The Builder Hub event lesson explicitly calls out AV quality as a common failure point and recommends prioritizing it.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community`](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community)
  - Signage, check‑in desk, nametags, swag (if provided through Team1).

### 2.2 Content & Speakers

Team1 materials emphasize accurate, up‑to‑date information when talking about Avalanche and encourage using official decks and resources where possible.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)  
Builder Hub courses, including Avalanche Fundamentals and Blockchain Fundamentals, provide canonical technical explanations you can reuse or reference.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/avalanche-fundamentals`](https://build.avax.network/academy/avalanche-l1/avalanche-fundamentals) [`https://build.avax.network/academy/blockchain`](https://build.avax.network/academy/blockchain)

**Checklist – Content Prep**

- Choose or adapt:
  - Official Avalanche or Team1 introduction slides (from internal resources or manual / onboarding references).
  - Technical content (if any) that matches your audience’s level.
- Confirm speakers:
  - Bios, talk titles, and timings confirmed.
  - They understand **Team1 values** and community guidelines.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)
- Prepare:
  - Q&A prompts and backup questions.
  - Demo environments or testnets ready if you’re doing live demos.

### 2.3 Promotion & RSVPs

The event‑planning lesson suggests partnering with other communities and using event platforms like Luma or Eventbrite for RSVPs, as well as tools like Galxe or Zealy for gamified engagement.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community`](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community)

**Checklist – Promotion**

- Create:
  - Event landing page or RSVP link (Luma, Eventbrite, or similar).
  - Visual assets following Avalanche / Team1 brand guidelines where provided in documentation.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)
- Share via:
  - Team1 Discord channels relevant to your region / focus area.
  - Partner communities, university groups, or local builders.
  - Social media (including any official Team1 channels if appropriate and approved).[`https://www.team1.blog/about`](https://www.team1.blog/about)
- Plan:
  - 2–3 reminder posts leading up to the event.
  - Clear description of what people will **actually get** from attending (skills, connections, opportunities).

### 2.4 Compliance, Safety & Accessibility

Team1 values safe, inclusive, and welcoming environments, a theme that appears in community guidelines and onboarding expectations.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)  
Builder Hub’s broader community guidance also emphasizes inclusivity and avoiding practices that make events feel gated or unsafe.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect`](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect)

**Checklist – Safety & Access**

- Confirm:
  - Any necessary permissions with venue / university / partners.
  - That there is a clear **code of conduct** (Team1 / Avalanche guidelines) and that organizers know how to enforce it.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)
- Consider:
  - Accessibility (stairs vs elevator, quiet spaces, online access, language).
  - Dietary needs if providing food.

### 2.5 Additional Requirements for Technical Workshops

The Team1 Manual includes specific **minimum requirements** for technical workshops that are sponsored and supported by Avalanche through Team1. These requirements ensure that participants can actually follow along and that the content aligns with Avalanche priorities.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)

**Topic & Audience Requirements**

- Have a **community interested in a specific technical topic**, such as:
  - Introduction to blockchain
  - Introduction to Avalanche and its ecosystem
  - Introduction to Solidity
  - Custom L1s
  - Interoperability with ICM and ICTT
  - Encrypted tokens with eERC
- For any of these topics, participants should be directed to the **Builder Hub** for asynchronous content that complements the workshop, as the manual explicitly recommends pointing learners to Builder Hub (`https://build.avax.network`).[`https://build.avax.network`](https://build.avax.network)

**Venue & Infrastructure Requirements**

- Secure a **partnership or venue** suitable for a hands‑on workshop, ideally:
  - A classroom or room with tables for laptops
  - Enough **electrical outlets** for attendees
  - A **stable internet connection** for everyone following the exercises

**Instructor, Marketing & Materials**

- Ensure there is at least **one technical instructor with demonstrable knowledge** of the chosen topic, as required by the Team1 Manual for technical workshops.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)
- Have a clear **marketing strategy** to attract participants and guarantee attendance (coordination with local communities, universities, online audiences, etc.).
- **Prepare educational materials** in advance:
  - Slides or written guides
  - Repos, starter kits, or example code (for Solidity, custom L1s, ICM/ICTT, eERC, etc.)
  - Links to relevant Builder Hub courses (e.g., Avalanche Fundamentals, Custom L1s, Interoperability, Encrypted ERC) to reinforce learning.[`https://build.avax.network`](https://build.avax.network)
