## Summary and next steps

You moved from “can I run one good night?” to “can I run a **program** that compounds?” That means cadence, shared ownership, partnerships that split work fairly, content that outlives the room, and metrics that show whether people **return and build**, not only whether they RSVP.

### Lock in three upgrades

Pick **three** items below, put dates on them in your calendar, and mention them once in Team1 Discord so someone can nudge you if you stall.

1. **Program arc:** Write a one-page season plan (12 weeks is enough): theme, audience ladder, and three anchor dates.
2. **Runbook:** Save a folder (Notion, Drive, or GitHub) with your last run-of-show, checklists, and partner email snippets labeled “reuse.”
3. **Volunteer lane:** Name one role you would delegate next time, with a one-screen briefing doc and a single point of contact for questions.
4. **Partnership:** List five orgs or communities you will actually DM with a specific co-host idea, not a vague “let’s collaborate.”
5. **Content:** Schedule two repurposing tasks in the 48 hours after your next event (for example short clip + recap thread).
6. **Metrics:** Add one **lagging** indicator you will report honestly in recaps for three events in a row (repeat attendance, workshop completion, or tracked Builder Hub course starts from your links).
