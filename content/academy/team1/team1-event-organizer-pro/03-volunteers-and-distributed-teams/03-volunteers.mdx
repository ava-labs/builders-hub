Volunteers are not “free labor.” They are **leverage** when expectations are explicit, and **risk** when everyone assumes someone else owns safety, check-in, or accurate messaging about Avalanche.

Community programs that scale often use a **snowflake pattern**: a core organizer trains leads, leads run satellite moments with the same playbook. You can borrow that without formalizing a whole chapter system on day one.

### Roles that scale cleanly

Start with a small RACI-style split. You do not need a corporate matrix, just clarity.

| Role | Owns | Should not own (unless trained) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Host / MC | Energy, timing, stage transitions | Financial promises to partners |
| Check-in | List, wristbands, first impression | Technical workshop debugging |
| AV buddy | Mic checks, slide swaps | Approving new talk content about Avalanche |
| Floater | Food, signage, quiet room checks | Final call on conduct escalation |
| Content capture | Photo list, short clips | Publishing partner quotes without approval |

**RACI reminder:** Responsible does the work, Accountable signs off, Consulted gives input, Informed gets updates. For Team1 events, **you** stay accountable to manual rules even when volunteers are responsible on the floor.

### Onboarding that takes ten minutes, not ten DMs

Send one message block before the shift:

```text
When: [arrival time] (not doors open time)
Where: [map link]
Wear: [plain guidance]
You will: [3 bullets max]
If something goes wrong: [one name + phone or Discord handle]
After: [where photos go, thank-you note timeline]
```

Add a single **“read this”** link: code of conduct plus one paragraph on **accurate Avalanche positioning** pointing to the [Team1 Manual](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual). Volunteers repeat what they heard in the green room. Make sure that room is aligned.

### Recognition that actually matters

- **Public thanks** in recap threads (tag handles people use professionally).
- **Skill-based appreciation:** reference letters for students, LinkedIn endorsements for AV help, GitHub co-author on small workshop repos when true.
- **Path to lead:** invite a strong volunteer to **co-MC** a smaller segment next time before handing a full night.

Avoid hollow NFT badges unless your crowd genuinely enjoys them. Many builders prefer **signal** (stage time, intros to partners) over trinkets.

### When to stop recruiting

Pause volunteer intake if:

- You cannot brief people before doors.
- Last event had more than one “I thought X was handling it” failure.
- Conduct or safety needs **senior presence** and you do not have it.

It is better to run a smaller clean event than a big fragile one.

### Questions to think through

1. **First delegate:** What is the first task you will give away next event, with a written one-pager?
2. **Escalation:** Who is the named person for conduct concerns, and have they agreed out loud?
3. **Quality bar:** Which volunteer mistakes would most damage Avalanche’s reputation or attendee trust, and how do you prevent them with five minutes of training?
4. **Burnout:** What signal will tell you the team is stretched (response time, snappy tone, missed checklists), and what will you cut first?
