## Welcome to Team1 Soft Skills

Technical knowledge gets you into rooms. Soft skills determine what happens once you're there.

This course is about the capabilities that sit underneath everything Team1 members do: explaining Avalanche clearly to someone who's never heard of it, running a workshop that people actually learn from, building relationships that last beyond a single event, listening well enough to keep a community from imploding, and leading people who nobody asked to follow you.

None of this is innate. These are learnable skills, and the Team1 context (global, decentralized, volunteer-driven, cross-cultural) makes developing them especially important and especially interesting.

---

## Prerequisites

You should have completed **Team1 Fundamentals** before this course. Not because the content here is technically complex, but because it helps to understand who Team1 is, what we're trying to do, and the kinds of situations where these skills show up.

No public speaking experience, facilitation background, or community management credentials are required. This course is for people at all levels.

---

## Course Modules

This course covers five areas, each focused on a skill that comes up regularly in Team1 work:

1. **Module 1: Communication & Storytelling**  
   Why experts struggle to explain things, how to adapt your message to different audiences, and how storytelling frameworks make complex ideas land.

2. **Module 2: Public Speaking & Presentations**  
   What makes a talk easy to follow, how to manage nerves, the real difference between a workshop and a keynote, and how to handle Q&A honestly.

3. **Module 3: Networking & Relationship Building**  
   Why collecting contacts rarely leads to real connections, how to have conversations worth having at events, and what follow-up actually looks like.

4. **Module 4: Active Listening & Empathy**  
   The difference between hearing and listening, practical techniques for online and in-person community management, and how to navigate difficult conversations without making them worse.

5. **Module 5: Leadership in Decentralized Communities**  
   How to influence without authority, coordinate across time zones, mentor new members, and sustain your contribution without burning out.

Use the sidebar to work through each module, then test your understanding with the quizzes at the end.
