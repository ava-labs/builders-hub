## 1. Making a Proposal

Team1 uses proposals to ensure events are aligned with Avalanche goals, make effective use of resources, and are set up for success.  
The Team1 Manual and onboarding hub reference proposal templates and approval workflows that members should follow when suggesting new events.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual) [`https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/Team1-Member-Onboarding-269688ec20c4808fb3ece079485f0441`](https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/Team1-Member-Onboarding-269688ec20c4808fb3ece079485f0441)

### 1.1 Start With Your “Why”

Builder Hub’s event‑planning lesson emphasizes starting with the **real** “why” of your event: who it is for, what outcomes you want, and how you’ll measure success.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community`](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community)

When drafting your Team1 proposal, clearly answer:

- Why this event, in this city / community / online space?
- Who is it for (students, devs, founders, gamers, local users)?
- What should attendees think, feel, or do when they leave?
- How does this contribute to Team1’s mission of organically growing the Avalanche community?[`https://www.avax.network/ambassador`](https://www.avax.network/ambassador)

### 1.2 One‑Page Proposal Outline

The event‑planning lesson recommends a concise one‑pager covering goals, target audience, vibe, and metrics; this format works very well for Team1 proposals too.[`https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community`](https://build.avax.network/academy/entrepreneur/web3-community-architect/07-community-building/09-event-planning-for-community)  
Team1 documentation additionally stresses clarity around logistics and responsibilities before committing resources.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)

**Checklist – Proposal One‑Pager**

- **Goal & success metrics defined**
  - Primary goal (e.g. “onboard 20 new devs to Avalanche tooling” or “kickstart a university club”).
  - 2–3 concrete metrics (RSVPs, attendance, number of builders reached, follow‑up signups).
- **Audience & format**
  - Who the event is for (students, devs, founders, general crypto‑curious, etc.).
  - Format (meetup, workshop, hackathon, online panel, office hours).
- **Program & speakers**
  - Short agenda (talks, demos, networking, Q&A).
  - Tentative speakers or facilitators (Team1 members, local builders, partners).
- **Date, time, and location**
  - Proposed date/time range and venue (or online platform).
- **Support requested**
  - What you need from Team1 or Avalanche (materials, swag, budget support, promo).
- **Risks & constraints**
  - Any known constraints (venue capacity, legal/school constraints, co‑host rules).

### 1.3 Internal Alignment & Submission

The Team1 Manual advises aligning with regional coordinators or working group leads before locking in larger events, especially when budget or strategic partnerships are involved.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)

**Checklist – Before You Submit**

- Share draft with:
  - Your **regional lead / coordinator** (if applicable).
  - Relevant **working group** (e.g., university, builders, gaming).
- Confirm:
  - That the event doesn’t clash with major Avalanche or Team1 activations in your region.
  - That expectations for budget, swag, and staffing are realistic.
- Use the **official proposal template / form** referenced in onboarding, not a custom format, so it fits into Team1’s workflows.[`https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/Team1-Member-Onboarding-269688ec20c4808fb3ece079485f0441`](https://avalancheteam1.notion.site/Team1-Member-Onboarding-269688ec20c4808fb3ece079485f0441)
- For the most up‑to‑date **“Propose an Event” link**, always follow the event‑proposal or request form linked from the Team1 Manual and onboarding hub, rather than bookmarking an old URL, since these forms may be updated over time.[`https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual`](https://aadao.gitbook.io/avalanche-team1-manual)
